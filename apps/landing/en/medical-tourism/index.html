Why Uzbekistan for medical treatment?

Uzbekistan combines several advantages for international patients. The country has a strong medical tradition dating back to the Soviet era — many specialists trained in Moscow, St. Petersburg, and now in Western countries. Infrastructure investment has accelerated since 2016, with new hospitals, diagnostic centers, and partnerships with international medical institutions.

Pricing is a key factor: procedures that cost $10,000–20,000 in Germany or $5,000–8,000 in Turkey can often be performed in Tashkent for $2,000–5,000 without compromising on quality.

Most popular medical tourism specialties in Uzbekistan

🎗️

Oncology

The Republican Specialized Scientific-Practical Medical Center of Oncology and Radiology in Tashkent is the country's leading cancer treatment facility, offering chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and surgical treatment.

❤️

Cardiac Surgery

The Republican Specialized Cardiology Center performs open-heart surgeries, coronary bypass, valve replacements, and pacemaker implantation at a fraction of European costs.

👁️

Ophthalmology

Laser vision correction (LASIK, LASEK), cataract surgery, glaucoma treatment. Prices 2–3x lower than in Europe, with modern equipment and experienced surgeons.

🦴

Orthopedics

Joint replacements (hip, knee), spine surgery, sports medicine. Growing number of orthopedic centers with international-standard implants.

🦷

Dentistry

Dental implants, prosthetics, orthodontics, veneers. Dental tourism to Tashkent is rapidly growing — prices are 3–5x lower than in Europe.

👶

Reproductive Medicine

IVF, fertility treatment. Qualified reproductive medicine specialists trained in Europe and Russia, at significantly lower cost.

Practical information for medical tourists in Uzbekistan

Visa. Citizens of 90+ countries can enter Uzbekistan visa-free for stays up to 30 days. CIS citizens enter without visa restrictions. Check the official Uzbekistan e-visa portal for your nationality.

Language. The official language is Uzbek. Russian is widely spoken by medical professionals. English is growing, particularly in Tashkent. AIVITA's medcard bridges the language gap.

Getting there. Tashkent International Airport (TAS) has direct connections to Moscow, Istanbul, Dubai, Beijing, Frankfurt, and other major hubs. Samarkand airport also serves international flights.
